## Main Direction

`Takedowns` should likely not be treated as one giant flat list of:

- setups
- ties
- true takedowns
- finishes
- mat returns
- sacrifice throws

It works better when split into:

- `Wrestling-style leg attacks`
- `Body-lock, clinch, and rear-body takedowns`
- `Trips, reaps, and foot attacks`
- `Core judo hand and shoulder throws`
- `Hip throws and hip-wheel family`
- `Major turning and lifting throws`
- `Sacrifice throws`
- `BJJ-specific standing entries often treated as takedowns`
- `Shared takedown families`

That matters because many names that sound like takedowns are actually:

- entries
- ties
- controls
- setups
- or finish options inside a larger takedown family

## Shared Families

### A. Leg-Attack Family

These all share the theme of capturing one or both legs, climbing the corner, and finishing before the opponent squares up.

- `Double Leg`
- single-leg variants
- `High Crotch`
- `Low Single`
- `Ankle Pick`
- `Knee Pick`
- `Snatch Single`

### B. Trip / Reap Family

These share the theme of blocking or reaping a support leg while steering the upper body.

- `Inside Trip`
- `Outside Trip`
- `Foot Sweeps`
- `O Soto / Ko Soto`
- `O Uchi / Ko Uchi`
- `Sasae / Hiza Guruma`
- `Valley Drop`

### C. Hip And Shoulder Throw Family

These share the theme of loading the opponent onto your hip / shoulder line and rotating them over it.

- seoi nage family
- `Kata Guruma`
- `Tai Otoshi`
- hip tosses
- `O Goshi / Uki Goshi / Harai Goshi / Uchi Mata`

### D. Body-Lock / Rear-Control Family

These share the theme of locking the torso first, then tripping, lifting, or returning.

- `Body Lock`
- `Bear Hug`
- `Rear Body Lock`
- `Mat Return`
- `Inside / Outside Trip From Body Lock`
- `Sag Body Lock`
- `Lift-And-Return`

### E. Sacrifice Family

These share the theme of giving your own balance to throw them over a controlled direction.

- `Tomoe Nage`
- `Sumi Gaeshi`
- `Tani Otoshi`
- `Yoko Otoshi`
- `Ura Nage`
- `Tawara Gaeshi`

## Important Note On Duplicates

A lot of names people treat as separate takedowns are better understood as:

### Same Takedown, Different Finish

- `Single Leg` → `Running The Pipe / Tree-Top / Crackdown / Shelf`
- `Double Leg` → `Blast Double / Snatch Double / Cutback Finish`
- `Body Lock` → `Inside Trip / Outside Trip / Mat Return`

### Same Family, Distinct Takedown

- `Ankle Pick` and `Knee Pick` are related, but not duplicates
- `O Soto Gari`, `O Soto Guruma`, and `O Soto Otoshi` are related, but not duplicates
- `Tomoe Nage` and `Sumi Gaeshi` are related, but not duplicates
- `Ippon Seoi Nage` and `Morote Seoi Nage` are related, but not duplicates

### Setup Confused With Takedown

- `Arm Drag` is often the setup; the finish may be a rear trip, mat return, or back take
- `Snap-Down` is often the setup; the takedown is usually the go-behind or spin-behind
- `Russian Tie` is usually the control; the takedown may be a single, ankle pick, or duck under

## Simple Way To Think About Takedowns

Most takedowns fall into one of these buckets:

- attack the legs directly
- trip or reap a support leg
- load them onto your hips or shoulders and rotate
- lock the body and return them to the mat
- use a sacrifice throw
- use a setup to create one of the five above

## Important Modeling Note

`Takedowns` should probably not be built as one giant flat list of:

- ties
- setups
- shots
- finishes
- trips
- throws
- mat returns

Safer structure:

1. `Core takedown` layer
2. `Takedown family` layer
3. `Setup / control` layer
4. `Finish / variation` layer
5. `Ruleset or stylistic context` layer when useful

That way the Index can support:

- cleaner taxonomy
- better cross-links to standing positions
- better relationship maps
- and future `Decision Tree` work

without treating every finish detail like a brand-new takedown.
