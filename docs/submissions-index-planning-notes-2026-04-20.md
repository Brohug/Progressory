## Main Direction

`Submissions` should likely not be treated as one giant flat list of every named finish.

It works better when split into:

- `Chokes and strangles`
- `Armlocks`
- `Leg locks`
- `Spinal / torso / body submissions`
- `Neck cranks and crank-style attacks`
- `Compression submissions and crushes`
- `Shared family themes`
- `Compact study categories`

That matters because many names are not truly separate submissions. They may instead be:

- the same submission with a different finish
- a variant inside a family
- a related but distinct attack
- a control position confused with a submission

## Shared Themes By Submission Family

### A. Blood Chokes / Strangles

These attack the neck and carotid structure more than the airway.

- `Rear Naked Choke`
- `Triangle Choke`
- `Arm Triangle Choke`
- `Bow And Arrow Choke`
- many collar chokes
- `Anaconda`
- `D'Arce`

Shared idea:

- cut blood flow by controlling neck structure and shoulder / head position

### B. Airway / Windpipe Pressure Chokes

These are often nastier and more direct on the throat.

- `Punch Choke`
- `Ezekiel`
- `Paper Cutter` in some finishes
- `Baseball Bat` in some finishes
- `Gogoplata`
- `Diesel Squeezel`

Shared idea:

- direct pressure across the throat or trachea

### C. Straight Extension Locks

These extend a joint in a direct line.

- `Armbar`
- `Kneebar`
- `Straight Ankle Lock`

Shared idea:

- isolate limb
- trap line
- extend hips or body against the joint

### D. Rotational Limb Locks

These twist the limb or joint line.

- `Kimura`
- `Americana`
- `Toe Hold`
- `Heel Hooks`
- `Wristlocks`
- `Omoplata` family

Shared idea:

- force rotational pressure when the limb cannot turn freely

### E. Compression Locks

These use a wedge to compress tissue and joint structure.

- `Biceps Slicer`
- `Calf Slicer`
- `Hamstring Slicer`
- `Texas Cloverleaf`
- some chest compressions

Shared idea:

- fold the body over a wedge and compress soft tissue / joint line

### F. Split / Groin / Spinal Attacks

These attack hip separation or spinal rotation.

- `Banana Split`
- `Electric Chair`
- `Twister`

Shared idea:

- separate or rotate the body beyond safe range

## Important Note On Duplicates

A lot of names people treat as separate submissions are often better understood as:

### Same Submission, Different Finish

- `Armbar`
- `Mounted Armbar`
- `Flying Armbar`
- `Belly-Down Armbar`

- `Straight Ankle Lock`
- `Belly-Down Ankle Lock`

- `Guillotine`
- `High-Elbow Guillotine`
- `Arm-In Guillotine`

### Same Family, Different Distinct Submission

- `Kimura` and `Americana` are related, but not duplicates
- `Anaconda` and `D'Arce` are related, but not duplicates
- `Inside Heel Hook` and `Outside Heel Hook` are related, but not duplicates
- `Omoplata`, `Monoplata`, and `Tarikoplata` are related, but not duplicates

### Position Confused With Submission

- `Spiderweb` is usually the armbar position, not a separate submission
- `Truck` is a control position, not itself the submission
- `Saddle / 411 / Honey Hole` are positions, not separate submissions
- `Crucifix` is a position, not always the submission itself

## Important Modeling Note

`Submissions` should probably not be built as one giant flat list of:

- choke families
- finishing variations
- control positions
- limb locks
- cranks
- compression mechanics

Safer structure:

1. `Core submission` layer
2. `Submission family` layer
3. `Variant / finish detail` layer
4. `Submission control position` layer
5. `Breaking mechanic / conceptual` layer

That way the Index can support:

- cleaner taxonomy
- better relationship maps
- better escape mapping
- and future `Decision Tree` work

without treating every positional finish detail like a brand-new submission.
