## Main Direction

`Positions` should likely not be treated as one giant flat list of everything that can happen in grappling.

It works better when split into:

- `Neutral / standing positions`
- `Top control positions`
- `Bottom guard positions`
- `Pins and dominant top positions`
- `Defensive bottom positions`
- `Transitional control positions`
- `Leg entanglement positions`
- `Submission control positions`
- `Hybrid or named system positions`

That matters because many names that sound similar are not duplicates. They may instead be:

- a position family
- a specific variation
- a transition
- a submission control
- a passing platform
- or an entanglement

## Shared Themes

### Guard Positions

These are positions where the legs are being used to manage distance, off-balance, attack, or retain:

- closed guard
- open guard
- half guard
- butterfly
- DLR
- RDLR
- X-guard
- SLX
- spider
- lasso
- lapel guards
- seated / supine variants

### Pin Positions

These are meant to immobilize and stabilize:

- side control
- kesa gatame
- north-south
- knee-on-belly
- mount
- back control
- crucifix

### Ride Positions

These are often more wrestling-influenced top controls:

- spiral ride
- wrist ride
- leg ride
- crab ride
- truck
- rear turtle ride

### Leg Entanglement Positions

These are control platforms for leg locking:

- ashi garami variations
- saddle
- sankaku variations
- 50/50
- 80/20
- backside positions

### Transitional Positions

These happen in motion and often decide who wins the next exchange:

- front headlock
- dogfight
- turtle
- inversion
- Granby
- wrestle-up
- sit-out
- Peterson
- chest-to-back scramble positions

## Important Note On Duplicates

A lot of names can sound like duplicates but are usually better handled as:

- position family
- specific variation
- transition
- submission control
- passing platform
- entanglement

Examples:

- `Mount`, `High Mount`, `Low Mount`, `S-Mount`, and `Technical Mount` are not duplicates
- `Ashi Garami`, `Single-Leg X`, `Cross Ashi`, and `Saddle` are related, but not duplicates
- `Front Headlock`, `Guillotine Position`, `Anaconda Setup`, and `D'Arce Setup` are related, but not the same position

## Important Modeling Note

`Positions` should probably not be built as one giant flat list of:

- standing ties
- guards
- pins
- rides
- transitional states
- entanglements
- submission control positions
- named passing platforms

Safer structure:

1. `Core position` layer
2. `Variation / sub-position` layer
3. `Transition / scramble state` layer
4. `Submission control position` layer
5. `Passing platform / system position` layer
6. `Leg entanglement / control platform` layer

That way the Index can support:

- cleaner taxonomy
- less duplicate confusion
- better cross-references
- and future `Decision Tree` branching

without flattening fundamentally different kinds of positions into the same level.
