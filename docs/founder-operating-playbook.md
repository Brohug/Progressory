# Founder Operating Playbook

Internal guide for turning founder interest into real gyms using Progressory.

This playbook is designed for the current stage of the product:
- founder-led sales
- high-touch onboarding
- manual approval before billing
- fast first value over perfect automation

---

## Core Goal

Move a gym owner from:

1. curiosity
2. founder interest
3. clear next step
4. owner invite
5. founder checkout
6. first real workflow in the app

The goal is not to show every feature. The goal is to get the gym using one useful workflow quickly and confidently.

---

## Founder Flow Overview

Recommended flow:

1. Gym owner lands on the site.
2. They either:
   - book a demo
   - apply for founder access
3. A founder request is captured in `public_inquiries`.
4. You review the lead and talk with them.
5. If they are a fit, provision their gym and owner invite.
6. They activate their account.
7. They start founder checkout from Billing.
8. Stripe puts them into `trialing`.
9. You help them get their first workflow live.

Important:
- founder access should feel personal and guided
- do not force a huge setup on day one
- do not try to teach the whole app in the first call

## Founder Call Script

Use this structure for a short founder call or demo:

1. Warm intro
2. Learn their current system
3. Find the real pain
4. Show only the relevant workflow
5. End with a simple next step

### Opening

Use something close to:

> Thanks for taking the time. I want to learn a little about your gym, how you currently track things, and then I can show you the part of Progressory that would actually be useful for you.

Questions:
- How big is your gym right now?
- Who would actually use this day to day, just you or coaches too?

### Current System

Questions:
- What are you using right now to keep track of classes, curriculum, or student progress?
- Is that mostly in your head, on paper, in notes, spreadsheets, or somewhere else?
- What is working okay with that system?
- What is the most frustrating part of it?

### Find the Real Pain

Questions:
- What are you trying to track that keeps slipping through the cracks?
- Where do you feel the most disorganized right now?
- If Progressory solved one thing first, what would you want that to be?
- Is the bigger pain class planning, student progress, curriculum organization, or coach consistency?

### Show Only the Relevant Workflow

Pick one lane:

- class planning
- student progress
- curriculum organization
- coach consistency

Use something close to:

> Based on what you said, let me show you the part that would help most first.

Do not show every page. Show only the workflow tied to their pain.

### Close

If they look like a fit:

> I think you would be a good fit for founder access. The easiest next step would be for me to set up your gym environment, send you your owner setup invite, and then help you get your first workflow running.

Explain simply:
- Founder Plan is `$50/month`
- includes a `30-day free trial`
- card is collected up front
- no charge until the trial ends

Then ask:

> Would you want to move forward with that, or would you rather do one more quick look before starting?

## Founder Approval Rule

Use this to decide:
- approve now
- follow up later
- pass for now

### Approve Now If Most Of These Are True

- they clearly have a tracking or organization problem
- they can describe that problem in real terms
- they care enough to fix it soon
- they are willing to change a workflow, not just browse
- there is a clear first use case
- they are responsive and engaged
- they seem able to pay `$50/month` after the trial if it works

### Good Founder-Fit Signals

- I keep losing track of what I taught.
- My coaches are not consistent.
- I need a better way to track student progress.
- Everything is in notes or my head.
- This would actually help us week to week.

### Follow Up Later If

- they seem interested but not urgent
- they are too busy to implement anything right now
- they want another look first
- they like the idea but cannot commit to a first workflow yet

### Pass For Now If

- they want heavy custom work before even trying it
- they want a very different product
- they are price-resistant before understanding value
- they seem unlikely to log in or use it

### Simple Internal Rule

- Approve if there is real pain, a clear first workflow, and willingness to try.
- Follow up later if interest is real but urgency is weak.
- Pass for now if there is no strong need or poor fit.

## Onboarding Checklist

The goal of onboarding is not to set up the whole academy.

The goal is to get to one useful workflow fast.

### Before Sending The Invite

1. confirm they are approved
2. decide their first workflow
3. decide whether they are starting with:
   - class planning
   - progress tracking
   - curriculum organization
   - coach alignment

### Provisioning Step

1. provision their gym
2. create owner invite
3. send the invite
4. tell them what happens next:
   - set password
   - log in
   - go to Billing
   - start founder checkout
   - begin setup with you

### After They Activate Their Account

1. confirm they can log in
2. confirm founder checkout is complete
3. confirm Billing shows:
   - `Founder`
   - `Trialing`
   - `Access granted: Yes`

### First Setup Session

Set up only:

1. gym/account basics confirmed
2. one program
3. a few topics
4. one real class or one progress example
5. one workflow they will repeat this week

### Example First Workflows

#### Class Planning First

- create one program
- add a few relevant topics
- plan one class
- show how to log it after teaching

#### Progress Tracking First

- add a few members
- connect one or two progress points
- show how they would update after class

#### Curriculum First

- create one program area
- add a few topics
- show how this becomes their reference point

#### Coach Alignment First

- show how coaches can use the same curriculum structure
- show how shared planning keeps classes more consistent

### First Session Success Standard

By the end of the first session, the owner should be able to say:

- I know where to start.
- I can use this this week.
- This already feels more organized.

## First Onboarding Session Script

Target length:
- `20 to 30 minutes`

### Opening

Use something close to:

> Now that you’re in, I want to keep this really simple. We do not need to build your whole gym today. I just want to help you get one real workflow live so this is useful immediately.

### Step 1: Confirm The First Workflow

Ask:
- What do you want this to help you with first this week?
- Do you want to start with class planning, student progress, curriculum organization, or coach alignment?

Pick only one.

### Step 2: Set Up The Minimum

Depending on their answer:

#### Class Planning

- create one program
- add a few topics
- plan one class
- show how to log the class

#### Progress Tracking

- add a few members
- connect a few progress points
- show how they update them

#### Curriculum Organization

- create one program area
- add a few core topics
- show how it becomes a coaching reference

#### Coach Alignment

- show how multiple coaches can use the same structure
- show how planning and topic visibility improve consistency

### Step 3: Use One Real Example

Use a real class, real student, or real curriculum section from their gym.

Do not keep it abstract.

### Step 4: Keep Scope Tight

Use something close to:

> We can build more later. Right now I want to make sure this first piece is something you’ll actually use this week.

### Step 5: Give One Next Action

End with:

> Your only job before we talk again is to use Progressory for [specific workflow].

Examples:
- Plan your next two classes here.
- Track these three students.
- Organize this one curriculum section.
- Have one coach reference this workflow.

### Closing

Use something close to:

> Once you use this once or twice, the next steps will make much more sense. We do not need to force the whole system on day one.

## Week-One Success Milestone

Pick one per gym:

- first class planned and logged
- first members tracked
- first curriculum section organized
- first coach-alignment workflow used

Do not try to hit every feature in week one.

The right question is:

> What would make this feel obviously useful this week?

1. lead approved
2. first workflow chosen
3. gym provisioned
4. invite sent
5. account activated
6. founder checkout completed
7. billing shows `trialing`
8. onboarding session completed
9. first workflow live
10. follow-up scheduled

## Suggested Founder Status Definitions

These are the most useful meanings for internal tracking:

- `new`
  - submitted, not yet contacted
- `contacted`
  - you reached out or had the first real conversation
- `provisioned`
  - gym and owner invite created
- `converted`
  - they are now a real customer or active founder gym

## Notes For This Stage

For the first 5 to 10 gyms:

- keep the process personal
- keep the setup small
- optimize for first value
- do not over-automate just because you can

The right experience at this stage is:

- fast response
- clear next step
- guided setup
- visible early value

That will convert better than a long self-serve process.
