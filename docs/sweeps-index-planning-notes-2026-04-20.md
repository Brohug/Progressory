## Main Direction

`Sweeps` should likely not be treated as one giant flat list of every named reversal from every guard.

It works better when split into:

- `Guard-specific sweep groups`
- `Shared sweep families`
- `Related positions and loading platforms`
- `Important variants`
- `Reduced core list`
- `Compact study categories`

That matters because many names are not truly separate sweeps. They may instead be:

- the same sweep from a different position
- a related but distinct sweep in the same family
- a loading position or guard that leads to a sweep
- a reversal mechanic rather than a classic guard sweep

## Shared Themes And Sweep Families

### A. Tilt And Off-Balance Sweeps

These rely on tipping the opponent when their balance line is compromised.

- `Scissor Sweep`
- `Basic Butterfly Sweep`
- `Hook Sweep`
- `Basic Lasso Sweep`
- `Basic Collar Sleeve Sweep`
- `Knee Shield Hook Sweep`
- `Basic X-Guard Sweep`

Shared idea:

- loading weight onto one side
- removing a post
- redirecting the upper body while controlling the lower body

### B. Elevation Sweeps

These lift the opponent's hips or center of gravity.

- `Balloon Sweep`
- `Tomoe Nage`
- `Sumi Gaeshi`
- `Elevator Butterfly Sweep`
- `Overhead Spider Sweep`
- `Overhead X-Guard Sweep`

Shared idea:

- placing hooks or feet under the opponent
- lifting them over your center line
- using momentum and angle rather than raw force

### C. Dump Sweeps

These force the opponent to fall by taking away a base post or steering them into a dead angle.

- `Tripod Sweep`
- `Double Ankle Sweep`
- `Ankle Pick Sweep`
- `Far Ankle Dump`
- `DLR Ankle Dump`
- `Outside Dump Single-Leg X Sweep`

Shared idea:

- controlling upper-body direction
- taking away one or both support posts
- toppling the opponent instead of lifting them

### D. Sit-Up And Wrestle-Up Sweeps

These come from rising into the opponent and connecting to their legs or hips.

- `Hip Bump Sweep`
- `Sit-Up Sweep`
- `Wrestle-Up Single Leg Sweep`
- `Wrestle-Up Double Leg Sweep`
- `Knee Shield Wrestle-Up Sweep`
- `RDLR Wrestle-Up Sweep`
- `Single-Leg X Wrestle-Up Sweep`

Shared idea:

- coming up to elbow / hand / seated posture
- connecting to a leg or hip
- converting guard into wrestling

### E. Underhook Half Guard Sweeps

These revolve around getting underneath or to the side through an underhook.

- `Old School Sweep`
- `Plan B Sweep`
- `Dogfight Sweep`
- `John Wayne Sweep`
- `Lucas Leite Style Coyote Sweep`
- `Underhook Half Bump Sweep`

Shared idea:

- underhook control
- getting on your side
- forcing opponent weight onto a trapped leg or hip line

### F. Spin-Under And Inversion Sweeps

These work by rotating under the opponent to attack the far side or backside.

- `Berimbolo Sweep`
- `Kiss Of The Dragon Sweep`
- `Baby Bolo Sweep`
- `RDLR Spin-Under Sweep`
- `Spin-Under X-Guard Sweep`
- `Collar Sleeve Spin-Under Sweep`

Shared idea:

- rotational movement
- accessing the far hip or backside
- winning angle rather than direct lifting

### G. Deep Half / Waiter Style Sweeps

These place you under the hips and use one leg as a loading point.

- `Waiter Sweep`
- `Deep Half Waiter Sweep`
- `Basic Deep Half Sweep`
- `Overhead Deep Half Sweep`
- `Homer Simpson Sweep`

Shared idea:

- getting underneath the center of gravity
- isolating a leg
- turning the opponent over a trapped line

### H. Submission-Threat Sweeps

These use a submission setup to expose balance and create the sweep.

- `Guillotine Sweep`
- `Kimura Sweep`
- `Omoplata-Related Lasso Sweep`
- `Front Headlock Butterfly Sweep`

Shared idea:

- threatening a finish
- forcing defensive posture
- using that reaction to reverse or come on top

### I. Reversal Mechanics

These are more scramble or bad-position reversals than classic guard sweeps.

- `Peterson Roll`
- `Sit-Out Reversal`
- `Peek-Out Reversal`
- `Bridge-And-Roll Reversal`
- `Scarf Hold Bridge Reversal`

Shared idea:

- escaping danger
- catching weight in transition
- reversing rather than sweeping from formal guard

## Why These Are Separate From The Sweep List

These are mostly:

- control positions
- entries
- loading platforms
- transitional hubs

They are not automatically the sweep itself.

Examples:

- `Butterfly Guard` leads to butterfly, sumi gaeshi, shoulder crunch, arm drag, and elevator sweeps
- `Dogfight` leads to dogfight sweep, back take, limp-arm come-up, or knee tap
- `X-Guard` leads to technical stand-up, overhead, backside, and tripod-style sweeps

## Simple "How To Think About Sweeps" Model

Most BJJ sweeps fall into one of these categories:

- tilt them sideways
- lift them overhead
- take away their posts
- come up underneath them
- get under the hips and turn them
- spin under to the backside
- wrestle up and finish on top
- reverse them in transition

That is why many sweeps feel related even when the names are different.

## Important Note On Duplicates

A lot of names people treat as separate sweeps are often better understood as:

### Same Sweep, Different Finish

- `Tripod Sweep` from open guard / DLR / spider
- `Tomoe Nage` from seated / DLR / spider
- `Waiter Sweep` from open guard / half guard / deep half

### Same Family, Different Distinct Sweep

- `Flower Sweep` and `Pendulum Sweep` are closely related, but not true duplicates
- `Basic Butterfly Sweep` and `Elevator Butterfly Sweep` are related, but not duplicates
- `Old School Sweep` and `Plan B` are related, but not duplicates
- `Berimbolo` and `Kiss Of The Dragon` are related, but not duplicates

### Position Confused With Sweep

- `Dogfight` is a position, not the sweep itself
- `X-Guard` is a position, not a sweep
- `Deep Half` is a position, not a sweep
- `Waiter` is often a position that leads to several sweeps
- `Berimbolo` is sometimes taught as a movement system, not just one sweep

## Important Modeling Note

`Sweeps` should probably not be built as one giant flat list of:

- guard categories
- named sweeps
- loading positions
- inversion systems
- reversal mechanics

Safer structure:

1. `Core sweep` layer
2. `Sweep family` layer
3. `Position / loading platform` layer
4. `Variant / finish detail` layer
5. `Reversal mechanic` layer

That way the Index can support:

- cleaner taxonomy
- better relationship maps
- better guard-specific organization
- and future `Decision Tree` work

without treating every setup or platform like a brand-new sweep.
