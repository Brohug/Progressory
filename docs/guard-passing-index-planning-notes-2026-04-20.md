## Main Direction

Guard passing should likely not be modeled as just a long flat list of named passes.

It works better as a combination of:

- `Core concepts / fundamentals`
- `Shared passing themes`
- `Named passing techniques / styles`
- `Passing study categories`

That matters because many passes are just different expressions of the same larger jobs:

- stay safe structurally
- win inside space
- clear frames and grips
- control hips and knees
- create angle
- pin the legs
- flatten the hips
- stabilize after the pass

## Shared Themes In Guard Passing Concepts

A lot of the fundamentals are different words for related jobs.

### A. Structure And Safety Theme

Used to avoid sweeps, submissions, and off-balancing.

- `Posture`
- `Base`
- `Balance`
- `Alignment`
- `Weight Distribution`

Shared idea:

- stay structurally safe while advancing

### B. Control And Immobilization Theme

Used to stop the bottom player from moving hips, knees, and frames back into you.

- `Pressure`
- `Hip Control`
- `Leg Control`
- `Pinning`
- `Shoulder Pressure`
- `Crossface Control`
- `Underhook Control`
- `Chest-To-Chest Stabilization`
- `Hip-To-Hip Control`

Shared idea:

- limit movement so the guard collapses

### C. Winning Space Theme

Used to get your body into the spaces the guard player wants to keep blocked.

- `Inside Position`
- `Head Position`
- `Connection`
- `Distance Management`
- `Angle Creation`
- `Wedge Creation`
- `Passing To The Weak Side`
- `Beating The Feet Before The Knees`
- `Beating The Knees Before The Hips`

Shared idea:

- occupy the space needed to dismantle the legs

### D. Breaking Defensive Structure Theme

Used to remove grips, frames, knee shields, and reguard tools.

- `Grip Fighting`
- `Frame Clearing`
- `Upper-Body / Lower-Body Separation`
- `Forcing Reactions`
- `Threat Layering`
- `Exposure Control`

Shared idea:

- make the defensive shell stop functioning

### E. Movement And Decision Theme

Used when the passer is switching sides, changing level, redirecting, or chasing the correct route.

- `Mobility`
- `Patience`
- `Timing`
- `Route Selection`
- `Transition Awareness`
- `Tempo Changes`

Shared idea:

- choose the right passing response at the right moment

### F. Finish-The-Pass Theme

Used once the legs are almost beaten.

- `Stabilization After The Pass`
- `Preventing Reguard`
- `Chest-To-Chest Stabilization`
- `Crossface Control`
- `Underhook Control`

Shared idea:

- the pass is not done until control is secured

## Shared Themes In Passing Techniques

Many techniques are different, but they group together.

### A. Speed / Loose Passing Theme

Used to move around the legs before strong connection is established.

- `Toreando Pass`
- `Bullfighter Pass`
- `X-Pass`
- `Throw-By Pass`
- `Side-To-Side Speed Pass`
- `Cartwheel Pass`
- `Cross-Step Pass`

Shared idea:

- beat the feet and redirect the legs before hooks or frames settle in

### B. Pressure / Smash Passing Theme

Used to compress hips and knees and force slower, heavier control.

- `Knee Cut Pass`
- `Side Smash Pass`
- `Pressure Pass`
- `Half Guard Smash Pass`
- `Hip Smash Pass`
- `Smash Knee Shield Pass`
- `Sao Paulo Pass`
- `Mounting Pass`

Shared idea:

- collapse frames and flatten hips with upper-body pressure

### C. Leg Pin / Angle Passing Theme

Used to trap one or both legs so the bottom player cannot square back up.

- `Leg Drag Pass`
- `Shin Trap Pass`
- `Staple Pass`
- `Shin Pin Pass`
- `Ankle Staple Pass`
- `Chest-Over-Shin Pass`
- `Leg Weave Pass`

Shared idea:

- pin the legs out of alignment so reguard becomes hard

### D. Under-The-Legs Passing Theme

Used to lift, stack, or connect under the hips / legs.

- `Over-Under Pass`
- `Double Under Pass`
- `Stack Pass`
- `Folding Pass`
- `Double-Leg Stack Pass`
- `Scoop Pass`

Shared idea:

- control the hips through the legs and force compression

### E. Tight Connection / Torso Control Passing Theme

Used to pass while glued tightly to the opponent.

- `Body Lock Pass`
- `Folding Body Lock Pass`
- `Near-Side Underhook Pass`
- `Crossface Underhook Half Guard Pass`
- `Headquarters Pass`
- `Backpack / Folding Headquarters Pass`

Shared idea:

- win the torso and hips together, then walk around the legs

### F. Direction-Change / Misdirection Theme

Used when one passing lane opens another.

- `Long Step Pass`
- `Backstep Pass`
- `Hip Switch Pass`
- `Float Pass`
- `Ghost-Style Float Pass`
- `Reverse Half Guard Pass`
- `Half Guard Windshield-Wiper Pass`

Shared idea:

- change direction faster than the guard player can square up

## Simple Master View Of Why Passing Works

Most good passing is one of these:

- `Go Around The Legs`
- `Go Through The Legs`
- `Go Under The Legs`
- `Pin The Legs First`
- `Flatten The Hips First`
- `Force A Reaction, Then Pass The Opening`

That is why many passing techniques feel different but are often built on the same core ideas.

## Important Modeling Note

Guard passing should probably not be built as one giant flat list of:

- concepts
- styles
- named passes
- study categories

Safer structure:

1. `Concept / Fundamentals` layer
2. `Technique / Style` layer
3. `Shared themes` as relationship glue
4. `Study categories` as curriculum organization

That way the Index can support:

- conceptual learning
- route selection
- style grouping
- and later `Decision Tree` branching

without duplicating the same information too many times.
